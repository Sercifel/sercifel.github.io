Motek 2026: Stuttgart’s Automation Week Hub with Bondexpo and Multi-Show Access

Oct 06, 2026

Motek 2026 runs 6–8 October 2026 at Messe Stuttgart, pairing production and assembly automation with Bondexpo and a one-ticket, multi-show format that targets integrators, OEMs, and factory operators.

Event Overview

Motek 2026 will take place 6 to 8 October 2026 at Messe Stuttgart, and it is positioned as the 43rd edition of the international trade fair for production and assembly automation. Unlike broader industrial expos, Motek focuses on the practical mechanics of making and assembling products at scale, including feed technology, material flow, handling technology, and industrial handling. The official positioning highlights “intelligent solutions diversity” in automation, which signals a show built around implementable systems rather than purely conceptual innovation. For buyers, this positioning matters because the automation stack is often a bottleneck in manufacturing projects, and Motek’s scope aligns with real integration challenges such as throughput balancing, handling reliability, and line flexibility across multiple product variants. For suppliers, it means the audience is likely to include engineers and decision makers who are actively evaluating deployment-ready automation components.

Motek + Bondexpo: A Dual-Show, Process-Chain Lens

Motek is staged together with Bondexpo, the trade fair for bonding technology, and the organizer positions the two events as a trade fair association that strengthens the process chain from handling and assembly to joining and bonding. This pairing creates a more complete manufacturing lens, especially for industries where assembly, fixing, and joining processes are tightly coupled with automation and material flow. The combination also changes the buyer mix: in addition to automation and handling specialists, the co-located Bondexpo attracts process engineers and materials specialists who need to align joining methods with automation systems. The show’s positioning thus becomes less “one technology category” and more “factory integration,” which is important for integrators who need to validate compatibility across subsystems. In practical terms, Motek + Bondexpo encourages buyers to evaluate automation and joining technologies together, which reduces the risk of siloed procurement.

One Ticket, Six Trade Fairs: Stuttgart’s Manufacturing Week Effect

A distinctive feature of Motek 2026 is the “one ticket – six trade fairs” model promoted by the organizer, which grants admission to additional trade fairs at the Stuttgart Exhibition Center during the same period. This transforms Motek from a single-event visit into a broader manufacturing week context, where buyers can compare automation offerings against adjacent categories and technologies without leaving the venue. For manufacturing companies, this matters because project decisions often require input from multiple functional teams, and a multi-show environment allows procurement, production engineering, and quality teams to see overlapping solution sets in one trip. The model also benefits suppliers by increasing cross‑industry traffic and exposure to visitors who may not have traveled for Motek alone. This multi‑show access is a concrete differentiator that makes Stuttgart a concentrated evaluation window for European manufacturing stakeholders.

Previous Edition Context: Scale and Momentum

The organizer’s coverage of Motek/Bondexpo 2024 provides a clear benchmark for scale and international participation, noting that 409 exhibitors from 19 countries showcased innovations in production automation. This figure is useful as a market signal because it indicates that the show attracts a substantial number of suppliers across multiple automation disciplines, not just a narrow slice of the industry. The 2024 coverage also frames Motek/Bondexpo as a platform for practical automation solutions, reinforcing the show’s emphasis on real deployment rather than abstract technology. For buyers, the 2024 scale suggests that Motek is large enough to support multi-vendor comparison within a single visit, which is critical for line integration decisions and vendor shortlisting. For suppliers, it signals a venue where qualified visitors are present and where products can be compared in direct competitive context.

Buyer and Supplier Value

From a buyer perspective, Motek 2026 offers a concentrated view of automation technologies that govern the most common production bottlenecks: feeding parts, moving materials, handling components, and assembling reliably at scale. The show’s scope fits companies that need to modernize production lines without overhauling entire plants, because it focuses on modular automation, material flow, and handling systems that can be integrated into existing operations. For suppliers, Motek delivers access to users who are typically deep in implementation planning rather than early discovery, which makes the show valuable for solution validation and integration discussions. The concurrent Bondexpo fair adds a second dimension by bringing in joining and bonding specialists, which can help automation suppliers align their systems with downstream process requirements. In short, Motek works as a targeted marketplace for production‑line optimization rather than a generic automation showcase.

Event Details and Sources

Motek 2026 runs 6 to 8 October 2026 at Messe Stuttgart, and is promoted as the 43rd edition of the international trade fair for production and assembly automation, with Bondexpo held concurrently. The official site confirms the dates, venue, and positioning around automation, feed technology, material flow, handling, and industrial handling, while the organizer’s 2024 coverage provides the most recent public scale indicator. These sources establish the show’s core scope, the dual‑event format, and the multi‑show access model that differentiates the Stuttgart exhibition week.
