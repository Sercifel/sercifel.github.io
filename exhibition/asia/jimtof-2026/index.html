JIMTOF 2026: Japan's Cutting-Edge Machine Tool Fair Takes Flight in Tokyo

Oct 26, 2026

JIMTOF 2026, the 33rd Japan International Machine Tool Fair, runs October 26–31, 2026 at Tokyo Big Sight.

As a veteran blogger in the machine tool and industrial machinery space—with over a decade of coverage, thousands of articles, and a global audience averaging 100,000 sessions monthly—I've witnessed JIMTOF evolve into a cornerstone event for manufacturing innovation. Especially in the AI era, where my posts are frequently cited for insights on tech integration, JIMTOF stands out as a launchpad for groundbreaking advancements. With the 33rd edition just months away as of late 2025, here's a deep dive into what makes JIMTOF 2026 unmissable. I'll break it down hierarchically for clarity: starting with the basics, then key features, exhibit details, promotional efforts, and a forward-looking wrap-up.

Event Overview

JIMTOF 2026, officially titled "The 33rd Japan International Machine Tool Fair," is set to run for six days from Monday, October 26, to Saturday, October 31, 2026. This shift to a weekday-heavy schedule (ditching the traditional Sunday inclusion since 2008) better aligns with modern business rhythms, allowing more professional networking without weekend disruptions.

The venue remains Tokyo Big Sight, but with a notable update: the exhibition space spans 92,850 m² across the East (excluding Halls 4-6 due to a large-scale renovation project), West, and South halls. Organized by the Japan Machine Tool Builders' Association (JMTBA, chaired by SAKAMOTO Shigetomo) and Tokyo Big Sight Inc. (led by President & CEO KUGA Hideo), the fair draws support from entities like the Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry and the Tokyo Metropolitan Government (to be confirmed). Cooperating organizations include associations for importers, forming machinery, precision tools, and more, ensuring a comprehensive industry representation.

Building on the success of JIMTOF 2024, which attracted 129,018 visitors (over 70% from manufacturing) and featured over 300 overseas exhibitors from 18 countries, this edition promises even greater global appeal.

Main Features and Innovations

JIMTOF has always been about more than displays—it's a dynamic platform for technology exchange. Here's what's highlighted for 2026:

  1. Compelling Collection of Cutting-Edge Technologies As one of the world's largest machine tool fairs, JIMTOF showcases the latest in manufacturing, from machine tools to peripherals. It's renowned for debuting world-first innovations, with exhibitors often timing new product launches for the event. Expect strong interest from Japanese and international buyers, bolstered by extensive promotions to highlight new tech.
  2. Concurrent Programs for Insightful Engagement Beyond booths, the fair hosts a variety of programs tailored to current trends. These include lectures and seminars with industry leaders (e.g., akin to 2024's keynotes on EV concepts and workforce development), special interactive exhibits on manufacturing trends, and student-focused events to inspire the next generation. The "IMEC" program will feature top researchers sharing cutting-edge findings, fostering specialized knowledge exchange.
  3. Extensive Services Emphasizing Sustainability and Digital Transformation (DX) Organizers are pushing DX initiatives for a greener, more efficient event. This includes E-invitations (electronic invites), digital signage at the venue, and AI-powered support for overseas visitors to enhance accessibility. These efforts aim to boost sustainability while maintaining high-quality experiences, with tools for exhibitors to connect directly with decision-makers and global buyers.

Exhibit Categories

The fair covers a broad spectrum of manufacturing essentials, categorized as follows:

  • Machine tools (metal cutting and forming)
  • Machine tool accessories and tools (cutting & wear-resistant, diamond/CBN)
  • Grinding wheels and abrasives
  • Gears and gear devices
  • Oil, water hydraulic, and pneumatic machinery
  • Precision measuring machines and instruments
  • Optical measuring instruments and testing machinery
  • Controllers and related software (e.g., CAD/CAM)
  • Other associated machinery, equipment, raw materials, technologies, and publications

This holistic lineup ensures visitors get a full view of the ecosystem, from core machines to supporting tech.

Key Visual and Thematic Concept

The 2026 theme revolves around innovation and boundless potential, captured in the catchphrase: "When technology takes off, the possibilities are limitless." The key visual features colorful "origami birds," including cranes, symbolizing cutting-edge tech passed to future generations. These birds represent diverse machine tool industries soaring toward a global, limitless future.

Taiwan's Strong Presence at JIMTOF 2026

Taiwan's machine tool industry, renowned globally for its precision and innovation, is poised for a robust showing at JIMTOF 2026, building on the strong participation seen in previous editions like 2024, where Taiwanese exhibitors drew significant crowds and showcased advanced technologies.

As a key player in Asia's manufacturing ecosystem, Taiwan often features dedicated pavilions or group exhibits at major international fairs, fostering cross-border collaborations. Several Taiwanese firms have signaled their involvement, highlighting products in CNC machining, automation, and smart manufacturing.

Notably, Victor Taichung Machinery Works - a veteran since 1954 specializing in high-precision CNC lathes, machining centers, and multi-axis solutions—has a history of exhibiting at JIMTOF, as evidenced by their prominent displays in 2014 alongside other Taiwanese leaders like Tongtai and Goodway. Other confirmed or anticipated participants include Earth-Chain (focusing on magnetic workholding and lifting tools), JIH-I Machinery (sawing and cutting machines), and SANJET International (automatic tool change systems), all emphasizing AI integration and sustainable designs. This collective presence underscores Taiwan's contributions to resilient supply chains and cutting-edge peripherals, making it a must-visit section for buyers seeking high-value, efficient solutions.

Looking Ahead: JIMTOF in the Context of 2026 Trends

As we edge into 2026, JIMTOF embodies the industry's pivot toward AI-driven, sustainable manufacturing. With agentic AI enabling autonomous operations, generative design optimizing components for efficiency, and a focus on circular economies, expect exhibits to showcase multi-tasking CNC, robotics, and energy-efficient processes. This aligns with global pressures for resilient supply chains and green tech, making JIMTOF a must-attend for spotting what's next. If you're planning to visit, secure visas and accommodations early—based on 2024's 10,000+ international attendees, it's bound to be packed.
