    Victor Taichung: Pioneering Talent Succession and Digital Transformation in Taiwan's Machine Tool Industry

    Mar 01, 2026

    Victor Taichung evolved from a small repair shop into a smart manufacturing leader, emphasizing talent development and digital transformation.

    In the dynamic landscape of smart manufacturing, where AI, automation, and sustainability drive global industries, Victor Taichung Machinery Works Co., Ltd.—widely known as Taichung Machinery or Tai Chung Precision Machinery—emerges as a beacon of innovation and resilience. Established in 1954 in a modest four-square-meter tin-roofed shed in Taiwan's "Cao Wei Zai Cheng" area, the company began with three partners repairing cigarette rolling and rice hulling machines. A pivotal moment came when they acquired a second-hand planer from a Kaohsiung shipyard, disassembling and reassembling it—a humble start that sparked a legacy in precision machinery.

    Today, Victor Taichung specializes in CNC lathes, multi-tasking turning machines, machining centers, plastic injection molding machines, and more, transitioning from traditional manufacturing to high-tech precision engineering. Beyond its technological prowess, the company excels in talent development, digital transformation, and sustainable practices, making succession and evolution replicable processes in an era of rapid change.

    A Legacy Rooted in Innovation, Quality, and Sustainability

    Victor Taichung's journey from a small workshop to a global leader in Taiwan's machine tool industry—ranking fourth in revenue among tool machine and component manufacturers in 2022—reflects unwavering commitment to "Technology Innovations and Quality Superiority." The company's state-of-the-art smart factory, inaugurated in 2020 at the Taichung Precision Machinery Innovation Technology Park, represents a $1.1 billion (NT$35 billion) investment in Industry 4.0. Inspired by a 2000 visit to FANUC's pollution-free, high-value facility at the foot of Mount Fuji in Japan, Chairman Huang Ming-he spent two decades realizing this vision. The factory integrates eight Victor Smart Production (VSP) assembly lines and four Victor Smart Machining (VSM) lines, featuring automated warehouses with 10,000 storage spaces, AGVs (Automated Guided Vehicles) from China Motor Corporation, and seamless ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning), MES (Manufacturing Execution Systems), and APS (Advanced Planning and Scheduling) integrations.

    This "black factory" turned smart exemplar eliminates information silos, enabling unmanned operations that optimize production schedules, quality control, and efficiency via industrial IoT. For instance, robotic arms handle loading/unloading with image recognition to prevent errors, while a seven-story automated warehouse boosts space efficiency by 300% and cuts entry/exit times by 60%, saving 66 labor hours monthly amid ongoing labor shortages. The war room, as the factory's nerve center, uses big data for decision-making, tracking utilization rates, tool health, alerts, and energy consumption—directly tied to accountable units for process optimization.

    Sustainability is woven into the fabric of Victor Taichung's operations. The green building, certified with a bronze-level green architecture label, incorporates rooftop solar panels generating renewable energy equivalent to the carbon absorption of 726 Da'an Forest Parks, underground conduits for natural cooling (reducing temperatures by 3-5°C in summer), ice storage air conditioning for off-peak energy use, and paperless internal/external processes. The company has outlined a net-zero roadmap: 25% carbon reduction by 2025, 50% by 2030, and net zero by 2050. Products like all-electric plastic injection molding machines save up to 90% water and 60% electricity, aligning with green design principles. As a leader, Victor Taichung extends these efforts to its supply chain, fostering collaborative ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) initiatives to help SMEs advance from Industry 2.0 to 4.0.

    Globally, Victor Taichung supports customers through an extensive network, including agents worldwide and eight technical support offices in the U.S., France, Germany, South Africa, Malaysia, Thailand, and China. This ensures efficient after-sales service for their adaptable, precision machines. Major clients like Foxconn (Hon Hai Group), which has purchased over 10,000 units, underscore the company's reliability—often spanning three generations of partnerships, despite premiums of 20-30% over competitors, thanks to superior quality and service.

    Bridging Generations: A Replicable Model for Talent Succession

    Recognizing that skilled talent is harder to replicate than machinery, Victor Taichung has built a robust system for nurturing and retaining expertise amid talent shortages and tech shifts. Over a decade of industry-academia partnerships with institutions like Taichung Industrial High School, Dajia Senior Industrial High School, National Chin-Yi University of Technology, Chaoyang University of Technology, and National Yunlin University of Science and Technology provide extended, hands-on training—from assembly basics to full machine logic. Housing and rental subsidies ease barriers, turning "local education, local employment" into reality, with a 70% retention rate and 150 core employees from this pipeline.

    The five-year-old "Mentor Group," comprising experts in mechanical, electrical control, and precision scraping, systematizes knowledge transfer. New hires receive five to six days of intensive training on skills, safety, and problem-solving, converting tacit crafts into structured modules. Competitions, like the "Victor Taichung Cup CNC Multi-Axis Machine Skills Competition," simulate production scenarios, filling national gaps in advanced skills and encouraging schools to upgrade. Internally, video tutorial contests build a 100+ course online library, promoting AI tool adoption and turning personal knowledge into organizational assets.

    Digital transformation further empowers talent. By recruiting IT specialists for cross-functional teams, the company develops in-house software for smart scheduling, fostering hardware-software synergy. Flattened hierarchies, dual-track promotions (technical vs. managerial), study groups, mini-EMBAs, and cross-departmental dialogues enhance learning and trust, boosting retention. As Corporate Sustainability Officer Huang Yi-ying (third-generation leader) notes, "People are the foundation of change. Even with AI, human application is key." Crises like the pandemic are viewed as training opportunities, emphasizing continuous evolution while staying true to origins.

    Brand Rejuvenation and Crisis as Opportunity

    Third-generation leaders like Huang Yi-ying, drawing from Mercedes-Benz marketing experience, have revitalized the brand. Overcoming the "dirty hands" stigma of manufacturing, initiatives include Taiwan's first machine tool Facebook page for real-time engagement, a mascot "Precision Baby" series, and collaborations with influencers for factory unboxings. Delivery ceremonies mimic luxury car handovers, with uniformed staff, branded vehicles, and exclusive gifts like mascot figurines—fostering loyalty and viral appeal.

    In governance, Victor Taichung transitions from family business to "family enterprise," assigning roles based on strengths: son Huang Hsien-pin handles sales and IT, leveraging his UK education and CNC certification to expand markets like Turkey (now 5% market share amid geopolitical shifts); daughter Huang Yi-ying oversees marketing and finance. This adaptive approach, combined with ESG focus, positions the company for long-term prosperity.

    Charting a Sustainable Future: Taiwan's Manufacturing Elevated

    Victor Taichung eschews flashy slogans for a clear roadmap: long-term academia ties, mentor foundations, competition-driven growth, digital empowerment, and green innovation. In smart manufacturing's tide, irreplaceable assets are talent cultivation and sustainable capabilities. By making succession and transformation replicable, Victor Taichung not only sustains itself but elevates Taiwan's manufacturing ecosystem—inviting SMEs to join via shared insights and phased upgrades.

    For deeper dives, explore their official about page, this talent succession feature, the digital transformation story, or the green factory insights. As industries navigate generational and environmental shifts, Victor Taichung's model offers a compelling blueprint.
