DETZO Introduces New Stator Winding Systems as Motor Production Demands Higher Precision and Stability

Apr 15, 2026

DETZO has launched the DSW-FM3002 and upgraded DSW-6012 series to address growing demand for stable, precise, and scalable stator winding in advanced motor manufacturing.

Motor manufacturing is moving toward tighter tolerances, more complex stator designs, and higher expectations for process stability. In response to these shifts, DETZO has announced two new winding solutions for 2026: the DSW-FM3002 and the upgraded DSW-6012 New Series. The launch reflects a broader industry trend in which winding equipment is increasingly judged not only by output, but by its ability to improve consistency, support larger stators, and maintain reliable long-term performance.

Why Stator Winding Stability Is Becoming More Important

As motor applications evolve, winding quality has become a more visible production issue. In many manufacturing environments, common process challenges include inconsistent wire placement, unstable tension control, reduced fill quality, and difficulty handling larger or more demanding stator configurations.

These issues can affect more than a single production step. When winding stability declines, manufacturers may also face lower consistency, added process variation, higher defect risk, and increased pressure on downstream inspection and assembly.

This is one reason newer winding platforms are being developed with a stronger focus on machine rigidity, precision control, and structural optimization.

Two New Winding Systems Introduced for 2026

DETZO’s latest release includes one newly launched model for mid-to-large BLDC stator winding and one updated multi-axis winding series designed for long-term operational improvement.

DSW-FM3002 Targets Mid-to-Large BLDC Stators

The DSW-FM3002 is designed for mid-to-large BLDC stators and is positioned for applications that require stable winding performance under more demanding production conditions. According to the company, the machine can handle stators up to 210 mm O.D., making it suitable for larger motor formats, including e-bike hub motors and UAV propulsion systems.

Its technical positioning centers on structural rigidity and precise wire control. DETZO states that the machine integrates Panasonic servo systems with Keyence control architecture to improve positioning performance in tight slot spaces and support higher fill-factor winding.

The model also includes:

  • automatic wire grooming
  • precision tension control
  • reinforced structural support for larger stators
  • improved winding consistency in confined slot configurations

These features suggest a focus on reducing process variation while supporting more complex stator requirements.

DSW-6012 New Series Focuses on Structural and Design Upgrades

Alongside the DSW-FM3002, DETZO has also introduced the 2026 DSW-6012 New Series, an updated version of its established multi-axis winding platform.

Rather than repositioning the machine around a completely new technical concept, the upgrade appears to focus on machine refinement. The updated series retains the core technical parameters associated with the previous DSW-6012PN platform while placing greater emphasis on industrial design, internal mechanical detail, and long-term operational stability.

According to DETZO, the update includes:

  • a redesigned industrial exterior
  • improved durability and visual integration in automated factory settings
  • optimized internal mechanical details
  • stronger long-term operational stability

This approach reflects a practical reality in motor production: equipment value is often determined not only by winding capability, but also by how well the machine performs over extended operating cycles.

What Problems These New Systems Are Intended to Address

The launch is notable because it aligns product features with widely recognized production pain points in stator winding.

Before evaluating the two models in more detail, it is useful to look at the challenges they are designed to address.

Production Challenge Reported Equipment Response
Larger stators are harder to wind consistently Reinforced structural rigidity and support for up to 210 mm O.D. stators
Tight slot spaces reduce winding accuracy Precision positioning through servo and control system integration
Tension instability affects fill quality Precision tension control throughout the winding process
Residual wire can slow downstream operations Automatic wire grooming for cleaner process results
Long-term use can reduce machine consistency Mechanical refinement and structural optimization

A Broader Signal in Motor Manufacturing

The release of the DSW-FM3002 and DSW-6012 New Series also points to a larger shift in winding equipment development. As motor applications become more advanced, equipment suppliers are placing greater emphasis on three priorities:

Higher Precision for More Demanding Stator Designs

Compact motor structures and tighter slot configurations require more accurate wire placement. Equipment that can maintain positioning accuracy under these conditions is becoming increasingly important.

Greater Stability for Larger and More Complex Applications

As stator sizes grow and application requirements diversify, machine rigidity and process stability become more central to production performance.

Longer-Term Reliability in Automated Environments

Manufacturing lines increasingly require equipment that not only performs well initially, but also delivers stable output over time with fewer mechanical inconsistencies.

These priorities are especially relevant in sectors linked to e-mobility, UAV systems, and other advanced motor applications where reliability and repeatability are essential.

Why This Launch Matters

DETZO’s 2026 product announcement is significant less because it introduces entirely new terminology and more because it responds to familiar production problems with targeted equipment upgrades. The DSW-FM3002 emphasizes larger BLDC stator capability and precision winding control, while the DSW-6012 New Series highlights structural refinement and long-term operational performance.

Taken together, the two releases suggest that the next phase of winding equipment competition will be shaped by a combination of precision, stability, and durability. For manufacturers monitoring equipment trends in advanced motor production, these are likely to remain key decision factors in the years ahead.
