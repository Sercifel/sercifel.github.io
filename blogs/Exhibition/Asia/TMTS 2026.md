# **The 9th Taiwan International Machine Tool Show (TMTS 2026): Focus on AI and Sustainable Manufacturing**

[![TMTS 2026](https://www.tmts.tw/storage/images/2ab0157c26753cd3d6a576307e636628.png)](https://www.tmts.tw/en)
The [9th Taiwan International Machine Tool Show (TMTS 2026)](https://www.tmts.tw/en), organized by the Taiwan Machine Tool & Accessory Builders' Association (TMBA), is scheduled to be held from **March 25 to 28, 2026**. The event will take place at the **Taichung International Exhibition Center (TICEC)**. TMTS 2026 builds upon the focus of the 2024 show, which centered on digital and green transformation.

The exhibition is projected to host approximately **400 companies** occupying **3,350 booths** across 28,483 square meters, and is expected to attract 45,000 visitors, including 3,000 international buyers.

**Core Theme and Strategic Pillars**

The overarching theme for TMTS 2026 is **"AI-Powered Sustainable Manufacturing"**, also expressed as **"AI Empowerment・Sustainable Manufacturing"**, which aims to showcase the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into production processes and efficiency optimization for smart manufacturing that is sustainable.

The event is structured around three core strategic themes:

1. **AI-Powered Sustainable Manufacturing**.

2. **Ecosystem Co-exhibition:** This involves a cross-domain connection of whole-plant machinery, components, AI, and green partners to establish a mutually beneficial smart ecosystem.

3. **Showroom-Factory Experience in Taichung (前店後廠):** This unique feature leverages the machine tool industry cluster in Taichung, allowing buyers to view innovations at the exhibition hall and then visit local factories to experience real-world product performance and manufacturing capabilities firsthand.

**Dual Transformation (DX and GX) Focus**

TMTS 2026 outlines [**10 key elements**](https://www.tmts.tw/en/theme) to guide exhibitors, aligning with the industry's shift toward value competition through dual transformation (DX and GX):

• **Digital Transformation (DX)**: This pillar includes **AI Empowered Manufacturing** (improving productivity and optimizing processes using AI), **AIoT** (enabling remote monitoring and predictive maintenance), **HRC (Human Robot Collaboration)** (integrating AI/IoT to transform production factors into data resources), **Digital Twin** (optimizing production parameters and reducing downtime), and **Big Data** (used for predictive maintenance, quality control, and optimizing marketing strategies).

• **Green Transformation (GX)**: This pillar focuses on sustainability through **Energy Efficiency** (reducing machine tool energy consumption), **Environmental Certification** (establishing protection management systems), **Resource Efficiency** (reducing waste via recycling and packaging reduction), **Low Carbon Supply Chain** (reducing carbon emissions through upstream/downstream collaboration), and **Sustainable Product Design**.

To actively promote these standards, the TMBA implements a "Dual Transformation Assessment" which awards the **"AI Empowerment × Energy Saving Label"**. The Energy Saving label recognizes green manufacturing results, while the AI Empowerment label certifies the introduction of smart technology and efficiency improvements.

**Impact of Japan's Strategic Investment Plans on Taiwan's Tool Industry**

Taiwan’s machine tool sector, specializing in high-precision and AI-driven solutions, operates within a region undergoing significant strategic economic shifts, particularly in Japan. In November 2025, Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi, who took office the preceding month, vowed to realize a "robust economy" by boosting investments across **17 strategic fields** through public spending.

![The 17 STRATEGIC FIELDS introduced by Japan's Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi in November 2025](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G5RJsWjXQAAEswT?format=jpg&name=900x900)

The Takaichi Cabinet instructed relevant ministers to draw up road maps detailing specific investment plans for these 17 areas, with the goal of finalizing the plan by summer 2026. Takaichi emphasized the need for bold strategic investments to enhance resilience and ensure both the public and private sectors work together to address social issues and risks.

Several of these 17 strategic investment areas directly overlap with the high-precision machinery and digital transformation capabilities of the Taiwanese tool industry:

• **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**

• **Semiconductors**

• **Shipbuilding**

• **Defense**

• **Aviation and Space/Aerospace**

Other strategic fields include quantum technology, biotechnology, and advanced medical sectors. Japan's commitment to injecting resources into these critical, high-tech areas is aimed at achieving a "virtuous cycle" of economic growth. This substantial government-led investment into sectors requiring advanced capital equipment, such as that showcased at TMTS 2026 (e.g., AIoT platforms, high-accuracy systems), establishes a significant potential market for Taiwanese machine tool manufacturers.
