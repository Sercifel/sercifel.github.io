# BI-MU 2026 (35th BI-MU): Milan’s Machine Tools and Metal Cutting & Forming Exhibition
## Event Overview

![](https://www.bimu.it/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/UCIMU_BI-MU_2025_HP_2_GB-800x524.png)

BI-MU 2026, the 35th BI‑MU, will take place 13-16 October 2026 at fieramilano Rho in Milan, Italy, and it is positioned as Italy’s leading machine tools and manufacturing technology exhibition. For a global industry audience, BI-MU 2026 can be described as a metal cutting and forming exhibition that brings together machine tool builders, automation providers, and industrial buyers to evaluate production technologies in one venue. The official site frames the event as an international biennial dedicated to metal cutting, metal forming, and additive machine tools, alongside robotics, digital manufacturing, and automation systems. This positioning makes BI‑MU 2026 relevant for buyers who need to compare equipment across multiple process stages, from core machining systems to digital factory tools and quality control technologies, within a four‑day sourcing and benchmarking window.
## 2026 Focus Areas and Thematic Structure
A defining feature of BI‑MU 2026 is its seven technological focus areas, which act as a practical map for visitors navigating the machine tools and advanced manufacturing ecosystem. The official list includes RobotHeart (Robotics & Artificial Intelligence), AMItaly (Additive Technologies), BI‑MU Digital (Digital Manufacturing & Adaptive Systems), Metrology and Testing (Control, Quality & Certification), Power4Machines (Mechatronics & Power Transmission), Heat and Surface Treatments (Finishing & Surface Treatments), and Tool Tech (Tools & Precision). The program is complemented by Revamping & Retrofitting, a dedicated stream for upgraded and regenerated machinery, which addresses the “second life” market for production assets. For buyers, this structure means BI‑MU 2026 is not only a machine tools showcase but also a focused way to evaluate robotics, AI, metrology, and digital factory systems that increasingly define competitive manufacturing performance.
## Index of Products and Category Breadth
The BI‑MU Index of Products confirms the breadth of the exhibition, listing machine tools for metal cutting and forming, welding and surface technology, additive manufacturing, composites, tooling, parts and components, manufacturing automation, metrology and quality control, digital factory technologies, subcontracting, services, and revamping/retrofitting. This taxonomy reflects how modern manufacturing decisions are made across an integrated production chain rather than by isolated equipment purchases. For a global audience searching BI‑MU machine tools or metal cutting and forming exhibition, the index signals that BI‑MU 2026 covers both the core machining categories and the enabling technologies that drive quality, throughput, and automation readiness. It also means the event serves both OEMs and job shops, supporting a wide range of investment priorities from high‑precision machining to system‑level integration.

## Buyer and Supplier Value
For buyers, BI‑MU 2026 offers a concentrated opportunity to evaluate machine tools, metal cutting and forming technologies, and the surrounding ecosystem of robotics, automation, and digital manufacturing solutions. The seven focus areas and the product index provide a structured path to compare equipment, systems, and services against production requirements such as accuracy, cycle time, automation level, and compliance. For suppliers, BI‑MU 2026 provides access to a specialized European audience actively seeking manufacturing upgrades, while the thematic structure supports solution‑oriented conversations rather than purely product‑level discussions. As a result, BI‑MU 2026 functions as a practical marketplace where equipment selection, integration planning, and vendor benchmarking can happen efficiently in a single event, which is a key value proposition for both buyers and exhibitors.
