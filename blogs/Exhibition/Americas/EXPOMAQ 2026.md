# EXPOMAQ 2026: Leon Becomes Mexico’s Advanced Manufacturing Showcase in June
## Event Overview

![](https://expomaq.org.mx/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/logo-400_inv.png)

[EXPOMAQ 2026](https://expomaq.org.mx/) will take place June 2–4, 2026 at Poliforum Leon in Leon, Guanajuato, Mexico, and it is positioned as a flagship event for metalworking and advanced manufacturing. The official site frames the show with the theme “Vive el mundial de la metalmecánica,” which signals an industry‑wide gathering rather than a narrow vertical expo, and the content emphasizes the full manufacturing ecosystem from machinery and tooling to automation and process technologies.

For a neutral industry audience, EXPOMAQ can be described as a regional marketplace where Mexican manufacturers, distributors, and technology suppliers connect to evaluate new equipment, compare process capabilities, and build partnerships across the metalworking supply chain. The show’s positioning and venue align with the industrial base in Guanajuato and the broader Bajio region, which makes it a practical destination for buyers seeking localized sourcing and manufacturing upgrades.
## Technology Scope and On‑Floor Focus

The EXPOMAQ site outlines the types of technologies expected on the show floor, and the descriptions suggest a cross‑disciplinary manufacturing focus rather than a single category showcase. The listed technologies include machine tools, CAD/CAM software, additive manufacturing, metrology and measurement systems, cutting tools and accessories, material handling, and robotics and automation. These areas collectively reflect how modern metalworking plants are built: machines, digital design and simulation, precision measurement, and automation are increasingly interdependent in production lines that must deliver both quality and throughput.

For buyers, this scope is important because it enables comparison of hardware, software, and process technologies in a single visit, which reduces evaluation time and supports coordinated investment decisions. The following list summarizes the show’s technology areas as described by the organizer:
- Machine tools for precision forming and cutting  
- CAD/CAM software for simulation and process optimization  
- Additive manufacturing for lightweight, complex structures  
- Metrology and measurement systems for verification and quality control  
- Cutting tools and accessories to improve efficiency and tool life  
- Material handling solutions for internal plant flow  
- Robotics and automation for productivity and consistency  
## Organizer and Industry Positioning

EXPOMAQ is organized by the Asociación Mexicana de Distribuidores de Maquinaria (AMDM), which the official site notes has more than 80 years of experience supporting Mexico’s industrial development. This institutional backing matters for buyers and suppliers because it signals a trade event grounded in industry networks and long‑term manufacturing relationships, rather than a one‑off commercial show.

The organizer also positions EXPOMAQ as a venue for high‑level industrial engagement, and the site highlights its role in connecting exhibitors, conferences, and specialized workshops. For an industry reader, the key implication is that the show aims to combine equipment discovery with knowledge exchange and operational best practices, which aligns with how manufacturing decisions are increasingly tied to workforce skills and process improvement initiatives. While the site includes many promotional elements, the AMDM connection provides a credible anchor for the show’s role in Mexico’s manufacturing ecosystem.
## Scale Signals and Program Elements

The official EXPOMAQ site highlights multiple scale indicators and program components that illustrate the event’s ambition and breadth, though the numbers appear as promotional signals rather than independently audited statistics. The site references machinery tonnage, international brands, and international pavilions, along with conferences and workshops, which together frame the show as a high‑activity manufacturing gathering rather than a small‑format regional event.

For buyers, the presence of conferences and specialized workshops suggests a dual track experience where technical learning can be combined with supplier evaluation, which is especially useful for teams planning equipment upgrades or process modernization. For suppliers, these program elements create additional touchpoints beyond booth traffic, enabling deeper technical conversations and positioning. In neutral terms, the site’s emphasis on both exhibition and conference programming indicates that EXPOMAQ is designed to support practical manufacturing decisions, not just product promotion.
## Buyer and Supplier Value

From a buyer perspective, EXPOMAQ 2026 offers an opportunity to compare multiple categories of production technology in one trip, which is valuable for manufacturers in automotive, aerospace, appliances, and general industrial supply chains that operate in the Bajio corridor. The broad technology scope supports procurement teams that need to evaluate machines, tooling, metrology, and automation in an integrated way, while the conference and workshop elements can help align technical teams on modernization priorities and workforce needs.

For suppliers, the show delivers access to a manufacturing‑heavy region and to distributors and integrators who influence purchasing decisions across Mexico’s industrial base. The event’s positioning around metalworking and advanced manufacturing also suggests a buyer audience focused on productivity, precision, and efficiency, which tends to generate more qualified commercial discussions. Overall, EXPOMAQ 2026 functions as a regional hub for manufacturing upgrades, technology comparison, and supplier relationship building.
