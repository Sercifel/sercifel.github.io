# Furniture Manufacturing Expo 2026: Charlotte Hosts the U.S. Furniture Supply‑Chain Marketplace

![](https://cdn.asp.events/CLIENT_Expositi_C43D86CE_5056_B733_493BD9501850F53D/sites/fme-2026/media/Branding/FME26_LOGO_DATE_2x.png)

## Event Overview
Furniture Manufacturing Expo 2026 will take place June 3–4, 2026 at the Charlotte Convention Center in Charlotte, North Carolina, and it is positioned as the only trade show in North America focused on bringing together the largest gathering of suppliers for furniture manufacturers. The official site frames the event as a two‑day marketplace where attendees can see the latest products, equipment, and machinery while also gaining insight into services and technologies that impact warehousing, logistics, finance, and operational performance. For a neutral industry audience, the show can be described as a focused sourcing venue for furniture manufacturers, designers, plant managers, and procurement leaders who need to evaluate both production equipment and the upstream materials that directly affect cost, quality, and lead time. The 2026 edition also marks a move to Charlotte, which the organizer highlights as a new location for the event, signaling a refreshed regional anchor for the U.S. furniture manufacturing community.
## 2026 Focus and Target Audience
The event’s official messaging emphasizes that Furniture Manufacturing Expo is designed for the full range of manufacturing roles, from executives and engineers to plant managers, machine operators, and purchasing managers. This focus indicates a strong production‑side audience rather than a retail or consumer orientation, which matters because decisions around machinery, materials, and process upgrades often require cross‑functional evaluation. The show is promoted as a place to generate new product ideas, develop supplier relationships, and strengthen existing partnerships, which aligns with the U.S. furniture sector’s need to balance domestic sourcing, quality consistency, and time‑to‑market. In addition, the organizer highlights an education program developed with guidance from an advisory board, signaling a structured knowledge component designed to complement the exhibition floor with applied insights and practical discussion topics.
## Exhibitor Coverage and Product Groups
The exhibitor scope is presented as comprehensive, covering both materials and manufacturing systems that support furniture production at scale. The official exhibitor product groups listed include adhesives, finishes, paints, software and information technology, contract manufacturing, cutting tools, fabrics, fibers, foam, hardware and mechanisms, labels, machinery, nonwovens, packaging, sewing equipment, springs, supplies and services, testing and laboratories, warehousing and logistics systems, wood and wood products, yarns and threads, and association publications. This breadth is important because furniture manufacturing relies on tightly coordinated inputs across fabrication, upholstery, assembly, and finishing, and the show’s structure provides a single venue to compare alternatives across these supply layers. For buyers, the category mix supports holistic sourcing; for suppliers, it creates exposure to multiple stages of the production chain rather than a single niche audience.
## Education Program and Knowledge Exchange
The 2026 education program is described as an engaging series of sessions guided by the show’s advisory board, with a focus on the latest innovations, technology, and techniques for the furniture manufacturing industry. This element matters because it positions the show as more than a procurement event; it also serves as a professional development platform where manufacturing leaders can align around emerging best practices and process improvements. The program is open to registered attendees without an additional fee, which can increase participation across roles and help create shared context between operational and commercial decision‑makers. For a third‑party industry reader, this signals that Furniture Manufacturing Expo aims to combine practical education with supplier discovery, enabling attendees to translate insights from sessions directly into discussions on the show floor.
## Buyer and Supplier Value
For buyers, Furniture Manufacturing Expo 2026 offers a concentrated opportunity to compare machinery, materials, and service partners in a single visit, which reduces evaluation time and supports more coordinated investment decisions. The show’s category breadth is particularly relevant for manufacturers managing complex supply chains, because it makes it possible to assess cost, quality, and compatibility across multiple input categories at once. For suppliers, the event provides access to a focused U.S. manufacturing audience, including decision‑makers who influence equipment purchases, process upgrades, and supply‑chain diversification. The combination of exhibition and education elements supports both immediate sourcing needs and longer‑term planning around technology adoption and process modernization, which is increasingly critical in a competitive domestic manufacturing environment.
