# Construct Iraq 2026: Iraq’s Leading Construction, Energy and HVAC‑R Trade Fair Returns to Erbil

![](https://constructiraq.net/data/files/logo/construct-logo_hvacraqlogokopya2.png)


**[Construct Iraq 2026](https://constructiraq.net/)**, the 8th International Trade Fair for Construction, Energy (Electric Power & Solar) and HVAC‑R Industries, will be held September 22–25, 2026 at the Erbil International Fairground in Iraq. Organized by So Fuar, the event is positioned as the country’s only UFI‑approved international construction exhibition and brings together regional and international suppliers across the full built‑environment value chain.

## Event Overview

The fair spans three core verticals—construction materials and systems, energy (including electric power and solar), and HVAC‑R—providing a single platform for developers, contractors, MEP specialists, distributors, utilities, and public‑sector buyers to connect with suppliers. The 2026 edition is expected to host 400+ exhibitors across approximately 17,000 sq. m of exhibition space, with a professional‑only visitor profile.

The show format is designed to cover both building envelope and infrastructure needs alongside power and climate systems: a relevant mix for a market that continues to invest in housing, commercial projects, and critical infrastructure. Construct Iraq is staged annually and has run consistently since 2018, reflecting continued market demand.

## What to Expect in 2026

Based on the official thematic layout, Construct Iraq and HVAC‑R Iraq are grouped in Hall 1, while Energy Iraq is in Hall 2. This structure reflects the fair’s two key buyer journeys:

- **Hall 1**: Construction and finishing materials, civil works, and building systems  
- **Hall 2**: Power generation, solar, electrical systems, and energy management  

Product scope on the exhibitor side typically includes building materials, insulation, waterproofing systems, doors/windows, structural steel, electrical infrastructure, lighting, pumps and valves, HVAC equipment, and solar components. The mix supports sourcing for both new‑build and retrofit projects.

The 2026 edition also arrives at a time when the Iraqi market is emphasizing resilient power supply and efficient building systems, making the co‑located Energy Iraq and HVAC‑R verticals particularly relevant for contractors, EPCs, and facility operators seeking integrated solutions.

## 2025 Edition Recap

The 2025 edition provides a clear benchmark for scale and market reach. Official figures list 350 booths, 16,189 professional visits, and participation from China, Egypt, India, Iran, Iraq, Türkiye, and the UAE. This footprint suggests the show has moved beyond a purely domestic sourcing event into a regional marketplace where international suppliers and Iraqi buyers transact side by side.

External listings also describe Construct Iraq as the largest construction‑focused event in the country and note its role as a meeting point for international companies entering the Iraqi market. These signals align with the show’s consistent growth since its 2018 launch.

## Exhibitor Highlights (Based on Recent Editions)

While the 2026 exhibitor list will finalize closer to the event, recent editions offer a snapshot of the range of suppliers typically present. Examples include:

- **SILKCOAT BOYA** (construction coatings/finishes)  
- **EONCRED GROUP** (decorative hardware and building components)  
- **CL Pipe & Plastic** (piping systems and plastics)  

Recent exhibitor catalogs also show participation from companies across solar energy, electrical systems, water treatment, and construction chemicals—indicating a balanced mix of core materials suppliers and technology‑driven solution providers.

## Why It Matters for Buyers and Suppliers

For Iraqi buyers, Construct Iraq serves as a consolidated sourcing venue for building materials, MEP systems, and energy solutions—especially valuable for projects that require coordinated procurement across construction and power/MEP disciplines. For international suppliers, the fair offers direct access to Iraqi contractors, developers, and public‑sector decision makers, with Erbil’s location making it a practical entry point into the wider market.

The event’s professional‑only visitor policy also reinforces its business‑first positioning, which tends to improve the quality of leads and on‑site negotiations for exhibitors.

- **Venue**: Erbil International Fairground, Iraq
