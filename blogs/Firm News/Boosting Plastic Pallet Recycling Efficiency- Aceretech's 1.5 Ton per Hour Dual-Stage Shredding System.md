# Boosting Plastic Pallet Recycling Efficiency: Aceretech's 1.5 Ton/Hour Dual-Stage Shredding System

Recycling bulky waste plastic pallets presents significant hurdles, such as inefficiency from jamming in single crushers, accelerated equipment wear, and inconsistent particle sizes that complicate downstream processing. Aceretech's innovative two-stage solution—combining a dual-shaft shredder and GH crusher—delivers a reliable 1.5 tons per hour output, ideal for medium to large-scale operations.

This integrated [**Recycling Pelletizing System**](https://www.aceretech.com/shop/category/recycling-pelletizing-system-4) begins with pre-shredding via the dual-shaft shredder, featuring a 1500mm rotor shaft and low-speed, high-torque counter-rotating knives. It breaks pallets into 50-150mm pieces, reducing volume and preventing overloads.

The second stage employs the GH crusher, with a 700mm rotor diameter and 1400mm shaft length, using high-speed impact and shearing for uniform 40-80mm granules. This prepares materials for efficient cleaning, separation, and granulation.

Trends include increased adoption of recycled HDPE/PP pallets in food & beverage, pharmaceuticals, and logistics due to hygiene, durability, and eco-regulations. Many new plastic pallets now incorporate high recycled content (up to 90% in some cases), supporting circular models where end-of-life pallets are fully recyclable into new ones. This shift reduces virgin plastic use, lowers waste, and aligns with global sustainability goals.
