# The Impact of Electric Vehicles on Garage Equipment Trends in Southeast Asia

![](http://products.com.tw/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/car-lift-for-electric-vehicles.png)

The automotive market in Thailand and Vietnam is changing fast. With governments pushing for cleaner energy, Electric Vehicles (EVs) are becoming common on the roads. For garage equipment distributors, this shift brings a new challenge.

Are standard car lifts ready for EVs?

The short answer is: mostly no. EVs are heavier and have different structures than gas cars. To service these vehicles safely, workshops need lifts with higher weight capacities and specific arm designs that protect the battery pack.

This article explains why the rise of EVs requires a new standard for lifting equipment.

## The Weight Problem: Batteries are Heavy

An electric car is much heavier than a petrol car of the same size. The battery pack alone can weigh several hundred kilograms.

Many older lifts in workshops were designed for lighter vehicles. Using an old lift for a heavy EV can be dangerous. It puts too much stress on the hydraulic system and the safety locks.

Distributors need to supply "Heavy-Duty" lifts. These machines have stronger steel structures and more powerful hydraulic cylinders. They are built to handle the extra weight safely.

## Lifting Points and Battery Protection

The bottom of a gas car has an exhaust pipe and a fuel tank. The bottom of an EV is a large, flat battery.

This changes how a mechanic lifts the car. If the lift arms are placed incorrectly, they can crush the battery casing. This is a very expensive mistake and can cause a fire.

Modern lifts designed for EVs have wider arm adjustments. They also come with special rubber pads or adapters. These features allow the mechanic to find the safe lifting points without touching the battery.

## Stability and Center of Gravity

EVs have a different center of gravity because the battery is located low and in the middle.

When an EV is in the air, it balances differently than a gas car. A lift must be stable enough to hold this weight securely. Cheap lifts with thin base plates may shake or wobble. This is unsafe for the mechanic working underneath.

High-quality lifts use wider columns and thicker base plates to ensure stability, no matter where the weight is concentrated.

## Conclusion

The transition to electric vehicles is not a trend; it is the future. Workshops in Southeast Asia must upgrade their tools to stay in business. Distributors who offer EV-ready equipment will lead the market. Focusing on weight capacity and safety features is the key to success in this new era.
