Case Study: How Matila's CT-TL3 Co-Extrusion Blown Film Line Elevates Film Strength and Clarity for L’Entreprise d'Emballage de Tunisie

Jun 09, 2026

By transitioning from traditional mono-layer extrusion to Matila's ABC 3-layer co-extrusion technology, the North African packaging leader unlocked 20% higher tensile strength and a 50% increase in output, solving critical lamination and printing challenges.

In the rapidly evolving flexible packaging industry, global manufacturers are constantly seeking ways to balance operational cost-efficiency with high-performance film quality. The market demand for thinner, stronger, and more transparent films has never been higher, particularly for high-end lamination and high-speed printing applications.

To address these market requirements, L’Entreprise d'Emballage de Tunisie, a prominent packaging manufacturer based in Tunisia, modernized its production floor by upgrading to the Matila CT-TL3 Three-Layer Co-Extrusion Blown Film Line. This technical case study highlights how the transition resolved production bottlenecks and established a new benchmark for packaging film performance.

1. The Strategic Advantage: Maximizing Tensile Strength and Material Versatility

For users of multi-layer extrusion machinery, the primary objectives are maximizing the film's tensile strength and maintaining the flexibility to switch raw materials across different layers. Unlike traditional mono-layer machines that apply a single polymer blend throughout the entire thickness, Matila's ABC 3-layer co-extrusion technology enables a strategic approach to film composition:

  • Enhanced Durability via ABC Layering: By utilizing an engineered ABC structure, manufacturers can strategically position specialized resins where they deliver the most impact. For instance, blending LLDPE with Metallocene in the core layer (B) yields superior mechanical toughness and puncture resistance.
  • Material and Cost Optimization: The ability to alternate raw materials across layers allows for a customized final product. High-clarity LDPE can be utilized in the outer layers (A and C) to enhance surface visual appeal and gloss, while cost-effective or high-strength resins are concentrated in the core layer (B).
  • Product Diversification: This technical flexibility enables packaging producers to diversify their product portfolios, smoothly transitioning between high-end lamination films and highly specialized shrinkable packaging tailored to precise client specifications.

2. Advanced Engineering: The Matila Die Head and Gauge Control Systems

While 3-layer co-extrusion systems are available on the market, the specific mechanical engineering behind Matila's components differentiates its final product characteristics:

Precision Die Head Distribution

Standard industry die designs frequently suffer from polymer flow stagnation or layer interface disturbances. Matila’s proprietary die head design is specifically engineered to ensure a highly uniform distribution of materials across all three layers (A, B, and C). This eliminates common flow irregularities, ensuring that the unique mechanical and chemical properties of each resin blend are fully realized in the final film structure.

Superior Gauge and Roll Uniformity

To complement the die head's precision, the CT-TL3 line integrates a advanced horizontal oscillating haul-off unit. This specialized system maintains strict control over gauge variations, successfully keeping film thickness deviations within a tight tolerance range of ±10%. This extreme precision ensures perfectly flat, wrinkle-free film reels, which are an absolute necessity for flawless high-speed printing and downstream lamination processes.

3. Comparative Results: Quantifiable Performance KPIs

The installation of the Matila CT-TL3 co-extrusion line delivered immediate, measurable improvements across all Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) at L’Entreprise d'Emballage de Tunisie compared to their previous mono-layer setup:

Performance Metric Previous Mono-Layer System Matila CT-TL3 (3-Layer Line) Documented Improvement & Impact
Average Output 120 kg/hr 180 kg/hr +50% increase in manufacturing throughput
Thickness Variation ±12% ±10% Significant stability, guaranteeing flat film reels
Tensile Strength Standard Baseline 20% Higher Enhanced durability achieved at 15% less thickness
Haze (Clarity) 12% - 15% 8% - 9% 30% clearer optical quality for lamination
Energy Consumption High (Traditional Belt-driven) 15% Lower Substantial cost reduction via Direct-driven system

4. Real-World Validation from the Factory Floor

The operational advantages of the CT-TL3 have been validated by technical leadership in Tunisia, confirming that precision engineering translates directly into factory-floor efficiency:

"Upgrading to Matila’s 3-layer machine was a smart move. Their unique die head design provides superior layer uniformity without the usual flow problems. Achieving a ±10% thickness tolerance via the horizontal oscillating haul-off has given us the perfect film quality for further processing."

— Factory Director, L’Entreprise d'Emballage de Tunisie

5. Conclusion: Technical Excellence Driving Profitability

The success experienced by L’Entreprise d'Emballage de Tunisie demonstrates that upgrading to the Matila CT-TL3 series is a calculated investment in modern flexible packaging technology. By producing films that are 15% thinner yet 20% stronger—and boasting a 30% improvement in optical clarity—the company successfully minimized raw material consumption while meeting the rigorous quality standards of the global packaging market.

For global plastic manufacturers looking to elevate film integrity, reduce gauge variation, and lower energy usage by 15%, the Matila CT-TL3 series represents a definitive benchmark in technical excellence and sustainable profitability.
