Industrial Transformation Saudi Arabia 2026: Riyadhs Industry 4.0 Marketplace Returns with a Vision 2030 Focus

Nov 30, 2026

Industrial Transformation Saudi Arabia 2026 returns to Riyadh on Nov 30–Dec 2 at RICEC, bringing together automation, digitalization, 5G, energy, intralogistics, and additive manufacturing for Vision 2030-aligned industrial transformation.

Event Overview

Industrial Transformation Saudi Arabia 2026 will take place Nov 30 - Dec 2, 2026 at the Riyadh International Convention and Exhibition Center (RICEC) in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. Positioned as a flagship industrial platform under the patronage of the Ministry of Industry and Mineral Resources and powered by the Hannover Messe network, the event is framed as a deploy now marketplace where factories, EPCs, and systems integrators evaluate automation, digitalization, wireless and 5G, energy, intralogistics, and additive manufacturing in one place. The official event description emphasizes a practical, production oriented audience, rather than a purely conceptual showcase, which is why the program combines an exhibition floor with multi stage content and on floor zones. For international suppliers, the show offers a structured entry point into the Saudi industrial modernization agenda, while for local manufacturers and procurement teams it provides a concentrated view of the technologies aligned with Vision 2030 and the push to build smarter, greener, and more resilient production capacity.

2026 Focus and On Floor Zones

The 2026 edition is organized around a theme of "Powering the Future with Technology" and highlights the pillars of Intelligence, Sustainability, and Resilience, which the organizer frames as the practical foundation for industrial transformation in the Kingdom. The official exhibit profiles and technology pillars point to a focus on Automation and Industry 4.0, Digitalization and AI, Wireless and 5G and IIoT, Intralogistics and Warehousing, Additive Manufacturing, and Energy Solutions, reflecting a balance between factory automation and the digital infrastructure required to scale it. A key differentiator for this event is the emphasis on on floor zones and dedicated experience areas that provide live demonstrations and applied use cases, which helps buyers validate performance claims and integration readiness in a realistic environment rather than relying only on brochures or presentations. The following zones and special areas are highlighted in the official program and are intended to align product discovery with deployment needs:

  • 3D Printing Zone with materials and design labs and live additive manufacturing demos

  • Application Park Zone with cross industry digitization use cases and factory simulations

  • Robotics Experience Zone showcasing industrial cells, safety guarding, and vision demos

  • Startup Zone and Investor Lounge to connect scaling technologies with funding and localization

2025 Recap and Market Signal

The 2025 edition provides a strong benchmark for scale and commercial impact, and the organizers present it as a proof point that Saudi Arabia has an active market for advanced manufacturing solutions. The official recap reports 283 exhibitors from 18 countries, 30 startups, and 8,420 visitors, with 40 MOUs signed and a reported SAR 100 million in deals, which together position the event as more than a visibility exercise. The show also featured a Future Factories Program and dedicated Robotics and 3D Printing zones, reinforcing the focus on real deployment rather than pure theory. On the content side, the Future of Industrial Transformation Summit reported 110 speakers, 4 stages, 24 sessions, 30 case studies, and 10 workshops and classes, which signals a breadth of applied case studies intended for procurement, engineering, and operations teams. In addition to the official recap, external coverage such as the Saudi Press Agency reported that Riyadh would host the Industrial Transformation 2025 exhibition in December, reinforcing that the event is embedded in national industrial communications and public sector visibility.

Buyer and Supplier Value

From a buyer perspective, Industrial Transformation Saudi Arabia concentrates a wide set of industrial technologies into a single venue and time window, which reduces search costs and shortens the evaluation cycle for modernization projects. Buyers can compare automation platforms, digital factory tools, and energy efficiency solutions while also assessing integration partners and service providers that support deployment and lifecycle operations. For suppliers, the show offers access to a Saudi manufacturing audience that is actively seeking to deploy Industry 4.0 technologies in line with national policy goals, which makes the lead quality and buying intent a central part of the events positioning. The structure of the show, with a combination of exhibit pillars, on floor zones, and summit content, makes it easier to align product discovery with real deployment questions such as safety, interoperability, and return on investment. This is particularly relevant for EPCs and systems integrators who need to evaluate not just individual technologies but also how they fit into end to end production lines under Saudi regulatory and localization frameworks.
