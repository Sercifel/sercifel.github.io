interpack 2026: Dusseldorf Hosts the Global Processing and Packaging Marketplace

May 07, 2026

interpack 2026 runs 07-13 May 2026 at the fairground in Dusseldorf, bringing the processing and packaging value chain together with focus areas like smart manufacturing, innovative materials, and future skills, plus a Taiwan exhibitor spotlight.

Event Overview

interpack 2026 takes place 07-13 May 2026 at the fairground in Dusseldorf, Germany, and it is positioned as the global meeting point for processing and packaging across food, beverages, confectionery and bakery, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, non-food consumer goods, and industrial goods. The show is known for presenting the full value chain, from packaging materials and production to processing and packaging machinery, as well as related services, which makes it relevant to both brand owners and equipment buyers who need integrated solutions rather than isolated components. For a neutral third party industry audience, interpack is best described as a sourcing and technology benchmarking platform where packaging line owners, OEMs, and solution providers converge to compare equipment performance, assess materials innovation, and validate compliance and safety requirements across multiple industries in a single venue.

2026 Focus Areas and Hot Topics

The official interpack topics for 2026 emphasize Smart Manufacturing, Innovative Materials, and Future Skills, which together signal a practical shift toward connected production, materials redesign for sustainability, and workforce readiness for more automated packaging environments. These themes align with the challenges packaging buyers face today, such as improving line efficiency, reducing waste, and integrating data across machines and plants while still meeting regulatory and brand requirements. interpack also highlights sector specific coverage including food and beverage processing, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, confectionery and bakery, and industrial goods, reinforcing that the event is not limited to one vertical but instead supports multiple supply chains that share packaging technology foundations. The focus areas can be summarized as follows, while still reflecting the need for applied, production ready solutions rather than purely conceptual innovation:

  • Smart Manufacturing for connected, data driven packaging operations
  • Innovative Materials for performance, sustainability, and compliance shifts
  • Future Skills for workforce readiness in automated and digital packaging

Previous Edition Context and Market Signal

While interpack 2026 is the next major edition, the official interpack site and event communications frame interpack 2023 as a strong reference point for industry confidence, with multiple exhibitor testimonials highlighting high visitor quality, strong international participation, and practical business outcomes. In neutral terms, these 2023 impressions suggest that interpack is not only a showcase for new technologies but also a venue where procurement decisions are actively advanced, and in some cases equipment purchases are initiated on site. The structural data for interpack 2023 is published by the organizer and provides a formal benchmark for scale, exhibitor mix, and visitor composition, which buyers can use to contextualize the size and international reach of the event when planning 2026 participation. This combination of qualitative feedback and official structural data is useful because it indicates that the show serves both innovation discovery and transactional business needs.

Taiwan Exhibitor Spotlight: Selected 2026 Profiles

The Taiwan exhibitor list for interpack 2026 reflects a strong presence in packaging machinery and packaging design, and several profiles offer enough detail to illustrate specific buyer relevance. Pack Leader Machinery highlights a focus on packaging automation, with product categories on the interpack profile that include pressure sensitive labeling machines, liquid production line components, and primary packaging systems, and the profile text emphasizes addressing lead time stability and quality drift in packaging lines. ChumPower Machinery describes its specialization in linear stretch blow molding machines for bottled water production, with in house machining for molds and use cases across global customers, which makes it relevant to beverage producers seeking integrated blow molding and packaging partners. Oriental Packing positions itself around packaging design and structure, with product categories that include folded and rigid boxes, and the profile showcases a range of premium box formats that align with gift, cosmetics, and specialty product packaging needs. These examples show Taiwan suppliers covering both machinery and packaging design, helping buyers evaluate suppliers across the equipment and packaging format spectrum.

Buyer and Supplier Value

For buyers, interpack 2026 offers a concentrated view of processing and packaging technologies that can be benchmarked against multiple sectors in a single week, which is particularly useful for companies with multi category product portfolios or global sourcing strategies. The show enables packaging teams to compare machine capabilities, material innovations, and line integration approaches while also engaging with suppliers who can provide after sales service and compliance support for regulated markets like food and pharmaceuticals. For suppliers, interpack provides direct access to OEMs, brand owners, and contract manufacturers who are actively evaluating capital investments and materials upgrades, making it a high intent environment rather than a general marketing event. The presence of dedicated hot topics and special formats supports practical evaluation, allowing buyers to map vendor offerings to operational priorities such as throughput, waste reduction, traceability, and sustainability targets without losing sight of total cost of ownership and integration risk.
