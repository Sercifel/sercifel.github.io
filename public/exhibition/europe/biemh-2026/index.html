BIEMH 2026: The Gateway to Advanced Manufacturing and Machine Tool Excellence

Mar 02, 2026

The BIEMH (International Machine Tool Biennial) is one of the most significant industrial events in Europe and the undisputed leader in the Spanish market. Scheduled for June 2026 at the Bilbao Exhibition Centre (BEC), the 33rd edition of BIEMH will showcase the pinnacle of advanced manufacturing, metalworking machinery, and industrial digitalization.

As a biennial event, BIEMH 2026 is a critical milestone for the industry, representing two years of accumulated R&D and technological breakthroughs in high-precision engineering.

Event Logistics and Venue

  • Dates: June 2026 (Specific June dates to be finalized by the BEC organization)
  • Venue: Bilbao Exhibition Centre (BEC) (Azpeitia Kalea, 48902 Barakaldo, Bizkaia, Spain)
  • Scale: Occupying over 6 halls, the event brings together more than 1,500 exhibiting companies from 30+ countries.
  • Strategic Access: Located in the Basque Country, the industrial heart of Spain, Bilbao offers exceptional connectivity for aerospace, automotive, and energy sector professionals.

Core Exhibition Scopes: A Comprehensive Industrial Showcase

BIEMH 2026 is organized into specialized sectors to facilitate technical evaluation and procurement:

  1. Machine Tools: Advanced solutions in milling, turning, grinding, and electro-erosion (EDM).
  2. Additive Manufacturing (ADDIT³D): A dedicated hall for industrial 3D printing, featuring metal and polymer deposition technologies.
  3. Robotics and Automation: Focus on integrated production cells, end-of-arm tooling, and factory logistics.
  4. Tools and Accessories: Precision cutting tools, clamping systems, and modular components for CNC machinery.
  5. Digitalization (BeDIGITAL): Software solutions ranging from Digital Twins to Big Data and Cybersecurity for manufacturing.

Strategic Value: Why Attend BIEMH 2026?

1. Direct Access to the Basque Industrial Cluster

The Basque Country is world-renowned for its machine tool industry and aeronautics cluster. Attending BIEMH 2026 allows visitors to engage directly with the manufacturers themselves rather than just distributors. This provides a unique opportunity for deep technical dialogue and customized machinery configurations.

2. Live "Machinery in Motion"

A hallmark of BIEMH is the presence of large-scale machinery in full operation. Unlike digital-heavy fairs, BIEMH maintains a strong focus on "heavy metal," where engineers can evaluate machine rigidity, damping characteristics, and real-time cycle times under industrial load conditions.

3. Focus on Sustainability and the "Circular Machine"

In response to European "Green Deal" initiatives, BIEMH 2026 will feature a strong emphasis on the circular economy within the machine tool sector. This includes:

  • Retrofitting and Remanufacturing: Extending the life of heavy machinery through modern CNC upgrades.
  • Energy-Efficient Drive Systems: Demonstrating new power-on-demand technologies that significantly reduce the carbon footprint of production lines.

4. Synergy with Subcontracting and Maintenance

BIEMH runs concurrently with several specialized fairs, including WORKINN (Industrial Talent) and BEDIGITAL. This synergy allows a single visit to cover everything from raw material processing to the sourcing of skilled operators and digital maintenance services.

Planning and Professional Participation

Technical directors and procurement managers should prepare for a high-intensity event given the geographical spread of the Bilbao Exhibition Centre.

  • Registration: Professional accreditation is required. It is recommended to register early to access the "Biemh Visitors" portal for personalized scheduling.
  • B2B Matchmaking: The organization facilitates "International Buyers Programs," which help non-local companies connect with Spanish manufacturers for large-scale infrastructure projects.
